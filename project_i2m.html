I2M Associates

This is the website for the company for whom I currently work. It's a website for a small Houston-area environmental professional consulting company. They provide a diverse array of environmentally related services. I built it entirely from scratch.

The website is currently live at i2mAssoc.com.

I came to this project as the company was undergoing a change in structure, where the company, "Industrial Mining and Mineral", split into "I2M Associates" and "I2M Consulting". "I2M Consulting" kept the existing "I2M" website, and I was brought in to build the website for "I2M Associates". The former website can be found at i2mconsulting.com.

Working with the CEO of I2M Associates I started by identifying what they hoped to accomplish with the website: to bring in clients.

I'm a big fan of minimalism. I try to design by German Industrial Designer Dieter Rams' principle: "Less but better."

So what's the most critical information for anyone looking to hire an environmental consultant? Who you are, what you do, and how to contact you. So I put all of that information right up front on the home page.

Secondary to that we go more granular with what specific services the company provides and names and brief bios for the various consultants with the company (that page is not yet live.) The CEO also requested that I add a page to address the specific industry sectors in which they are qualified to work.

We also decided to include a page of abbreviated case-studies. It was requested that I create multiple case-study pages to handle different types of case-studies. I felt as though listing multiple such pages within the nav-structure would just make the site feel heavy and confusing, so within the "case-studies" page I created a table with a tab-structure. I like how it turned out, and now I have my own blueprint for tab-tables, without having to import a design library like Twitter Bootstrap. Unfortunately both this page and the "Associates" page remain under construction, so they are not visible on the website, but you can see a screenshot of the prototype "Case-Studies" page at the top of this article.
