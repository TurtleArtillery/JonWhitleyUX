Cost of Living Calculator

This project came about when I was once asked, as I’m sure you’ve been asked, “What is your desired salary range?” But it was for a city across the country and after swimming through salary comparison charts and “living-wage tables” I still had no idea. So I decided I wanted an app that would estimate what sort of life I would have on a given salary.

The Challenge: Design an app that would take a salary and location and provide a rough estimate of how the expenses play out.

The Solution: I think ultimately why there are no apps like this is that there are simply so many variables to consider. So I decided the app would default to average values for the location, (eg. Average monthly grocery bill for a family of 2 adults) but allow users to override these default estimates if they wanted a more accurate result.

To design it I sat down and ran through several calculations of my own to rough out what exactly were the expenses that should be considered. Then I tried to organize them into a logical structure. I organized them largely according to which categories would take inputs the same way and which needed special attention. For instance, I wanted Housing to be a separate tab so that I could be a little more flexible with the UI, since I wanted to have the option to switch between Apartment, House-Rent, House-Mortgage, and House-Own and each of those might have a different effect on the amount estimated in the Taxes section. I also wanted the option to add multiple residences and residences that aren’t necessarily in the same city as listed on the main screen. Overall, I’m pleased with the result.
