Cleanup Crew

The idea for this comes from a campaign I saw promoted when I was in Dallas: several posters advertised that you could take a GPS-tagged photo of garbage or graffiti and text it to the city and they would come and clean it up. I thought that was such a fantastic idea and I wanted to design a way to make it easily extensible to any city in the world.

The idea for this is to provide a way for people to report garbage or graffiti or whatever else you might find, even if you might not have the time or ability to clean it up for yourself. Anyone can report garbage or broken windows, and anyone can clean them up and clear them from the map. The ultimate goal is to gamify cleaning up the environment.

First things first, I sat down to really think about what needed to be included in the app. Actually, I didn’t sit down. I took a walk. I went out into the neighborhood and walked around just looking for things that were broken or otherwise in need of fixing. I found that there really is a depressing amount of trash just lying around. I actually saw trash (how shocking, right?), and broken windows, a broken street-lamp, and graffiti. Someone had thrown a shopping cart into the creek. I saw water welling up from a damp spot in someone’s yard where a pipe must have burst and it spilled wastefully into the street. These things informed some of the specific choices I made in designing this app.

For instance: critically there had to be a function for GPS-tagging; there had to be a function for identifying specifically what was gps-tagged. There had to be a function for viewing tags put up by other users and a function for dismissing tags that were no longer necessary. This was the core functionality of the app. I came to the design with three categories originally in mind: litter, graffiti, and wasted water, but as I designed I decided to include a few more. I ended up with eight – “Recycling bins”, “Trash-Cans”, “Graffiti”, “Litter”, “Damage”, “Bio-Hazard”, “Wasted Water”, and “Road Damage”. A posting user would then have the ability to enter a brief description of what was tagged, so that other users would be able to plan accordingly – for instance, if someone posts “Somebody dropped a broken refrigerator over the riverbank.” then other users know that not just anybody is going to fix that particular problem.
