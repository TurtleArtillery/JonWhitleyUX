Alchemist Health Providers

This was by far my biggest, most work-intensive UX project to date. It took weeks to create the full, high-fidelity prototype. That being said, I think it turned out well.

First things first, I noticed one day that while most hospital systems have decent enough desktop websites their mobile versions are almost uniformly lacking. I decided I could do better. The final product is designed as a basic blueprint that could be implemented by any hospital system that wished to do so.

Here's the final prototype - Alchemist Health Providers Prototype.

So my first steps were to research a wide spectrum of existing hospital websites and sit down and determine the most important parts of a hospital's website.

The most crucial user case I kept in mind was someone accessing the hospital's website in the midst of an emergency. I don't know if someone would actually take the time to visit a webpage in such an instance, but I wanted to prepare for that case nonetheless. So what does a user need most from a website in an emergency? It seems to me that clarity and a very clear to follow are the most important. So I placed the button for the emergency track in the place your eye goes to first and I made the button the only red item on the entire page, set against gray, black, and a darker purple. The next step along the line leads into a page with buttons for information or locations for emergency services and the ability to alert the hospital that you're coming. This last function is less a UX function and more a part of the greater Experience Design of the whole hospital system.

Beyond that I provide a direct line toward general hospital information, a path toward a portal for patients and another for physicians, a streamlined button specifically for finding a doctor, and a final button for looking up the hospital blog and hospital sponsored events.
